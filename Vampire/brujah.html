Brujah

The unattainable dream of the Educated Clan is a world in which all injustice will be eliminated and where the living and the undead will coexist in peace. They say they want this out of love for the mortals who led them against their masters. In truth, it is probably simple anger against a remote or non-existent God they can never face and against a curse they can never end. That of the Brujah is a dream that poisons everything it touches. As they infiltrate revolutions or instigate them from outside, their hunger and their passions are the guarantee that rivers of blood will be spilled, that the innocent will die and peace will never be will never be achieved.

The Brujah clan has always embraced those who show solidarity with the counterculture and revolution. It seeks allies who question normative ideas and, recognising the fire that ignites the oppressed, tends to gravitate towards the underdog. Even if the clan includes a not insignificant number of rowdy and all too visible outcasts, the desire for rebellion also lurks deeply in the conman who cheats his own company, in the lawyer who represents the poor pro bono, in the neo-Nazi who claims to belong to the 'alternative right' and in the basement tenant who downloads thousands of pirated films for redistribution on streaming sites. Brujahs can be passionate fighters, but also critical thinkers; the clan's activists are often very different from its theorists. On many occasions, the latter are embraced by former gender studies or sociology students, among those who have survived near-death experiences and individuals who have otherwise suffered severe personal losses.

The Brujah have always fought for the progress of the Brothers' society. Many of them were progressive while alive, and in the undead they continue continue to be so. The voice of the people can be a former feminist activist, an eco-warrior or an anti-capitalist protester anti-capitalist who channels his or her passion by speaking out against the Ventrue and the Toreadors or by fomenting political dissent among mortals. Their ambitions may backfire on the clan at a later date, but sometimes form the nucleus of revolutionary nucleus of revolutionary movements.

At one time the Brujah were considered a High Clan, superior to many and respected by all. Some of them still believe that their Blood is stronger than that of the other clans and that they have the right to impose their doctrine on others. The Brujah who support this form of blood idolatry often come from fundamentalist backgrounds fundamentalist backgrounds, fringes of the extreme right and from the academic elite.

The Brujah clan includes many activists who in word and deed uncritically follow the mainstream, basking in their own sense of righteousness. The Brujahs say that there is no clan more attached to humanity than them, and that is that is why they manifest such fiery such fiery passions. Some, however, go even further and try to live like a mortal while keeping up with modern culture, engaging in relationships and starting families. The reward for such behaviour is a staggering duality between dreams and reality. The Beast cannot be disowned for long, and increasingly more and more often the 'family' undead must slip out of their false existences to avoid seeing red, returning only after having satiated their dangerous needs.

Weakness

The Blood of the Brujah boils with fury barely restrained, ready to explode at the slightest provocation. A number of dice equal to the Severity of the Brujah's Weakness is subtracted from any roll to resist Fury Frenzy. The reserve cannot be reduced to less than one die.
